Shopping

Definition

"the action or activity of purchasing goods from stores."

Grocery shopping
Characteristics:
  • Price and quality varies by locations, ranging from farmer's markets to supermarkets.
  • Many brand selections for a single product, an overwhelming number of choices
  • Little to no social interaction besides paying for items

Shopping for clothes
Characteristics:
  • Price and quality varies by locations, formal manners required at high-end boutiques
  • Lots of nonverbal cues regarding sizing and aesthetics of clothing choice
  • High level of social interaction with employees, on topics such as style, sizing, and final purchasing.

Shopping for appliances
Characteristics:
  • Price varies by each product, depending on the specifications you want.
  • Direct communication with store employees
  • Medium level of social interaction with employees, only concrete topics are discussed.
Start steps diagram
Shopping for hobby/decoration items
Characteristics:
  • Price is correlated with quality, though not always; varies widely from big box stores to artisan shops
  • Specific, direct communication if you already know what you are getting, and the ability to give abstract descriptions of the item to store employees
  • Medium level of social interaction, an exception would be fine arts auctions, where strict rules only require lower levels of interaction.
