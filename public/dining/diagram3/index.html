Dining Out

Definition

"to dine away from home, esp in a restaurant. (foll by on) to have dinner at the expense of someone else mainly for the sake of one's knowledge or conversation about (a subject or story)"

Start the steps diagram

Start
If a family member is hosting a dinner party, make sure to bring a gift for the host. A bottle of wine or a bouquet of flowers is a good idea.
Give your present to the host when they first greet you. Ask them to be seated.
Take note of the relatives seated close to you so you can decide on appropriate conversation topics. For example, discuss school and toys with little children and steer clear of explicit material.
Once everyone is present, the food would be ready and the host would motion the guests to eat.
There are no set etiquette for family dinners, because it varies with different hosts. However, it is important to not chew with your mouth open and not interrupt others when they are speaking.
In conversation, set boundaries around what questions you are comfortable answering. You can opt not to speak, divert the subject, or simply say "I don't want to talk about it."
Make sure that you've greeted every relative before the dinner ends, to signify that you acknowledge every member of the family. Thank the host for preparing the meal and planning the party, and compliment their food regardless of how you feel about it.
