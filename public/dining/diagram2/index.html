Dining out

Definition

"to dine away from home, esp in a restaurant. (foll by on) to have dinner at the expense of someone else mainly for the sake of one's knowledge or conversation about (a subject or story)"

Start the steps diagram

Start
Make sure you and your friends have agreed on a time and place to dine out.
If you are going by yourself, arrive earlier and check for a table reservation under the group's name with the host.
Once everyone is seated and present, ask if everyone is ordering separately. If not, a member of the group will usually order large dishes to share, and you must go along with their choice. Otherwise, you can order as you please, waiting your turn.
Engage in conversation while waiting for food to arrive. Safe topics to discuss revolve around what the friend group have in common (for example, funny dorm experiences with college friends.)
Once the food is ready, start eating when everybody else does. Display proper table manners and do not talk while chewing.
Try giving compliments about the food you are eating. This will prompt a conversation where everyone shares their opinions about the food, and shows your gratefulness to the person paying the bill if you are eating what they ordered.
If the group decided on splitting the bill, pay your share in full. Otherwise, thank the person who paid for the table and remind yourself to repay them with a similar favor another time.
